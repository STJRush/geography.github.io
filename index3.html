Geography Topics!

What is on the course?

  • Strand 1- Exploring the physical World.

    Students should be able to describe the formation and global distribution of volcanoes, earthquakes and fold mountains in the context of plate tectonics and structure of the earth. They should be able to analyse different rock types referring to formation and composition. Analyse the process and effects of weathering and mass movement. Asses a soil type in a local soil type in relation to vegetation. Explain how the processes of erosion, deposition and transportation shape our landscapes. Analyse factors that effect climate. The formation of significant weather event. Record and interpret weather data. Differentiate between types of energy sources produced by the physical world, and lastly investigate a ranger of physical processes in a chosen location!

  • Strand 2- Social and economic impacts of dealing with natural disasters. Consequences of rock expoitation and energy resources. How physical landscape influences the development of primary activities. Assess water exploitation and fish stocks. Describe a local secondary activity in relation to its and the factors that influence its location. Causes and consequences of climate change. How people react to natural disasters. Assess the interrelationships between the physical world, tourism and transport.

  • Stand 3- Learn about demographic transition model to explain populations. Investigate causes and consequences of migration. Examine population change in Ireland. Causes and consequences of urban change in an Irish city or town. Global patterns of economic development. Compare life chances of the youth in a developing country to one in a developed country. Evaluate the role of development assistance in human development. Synthesise their learning of population, settlement and human development within the process of globalisation.
